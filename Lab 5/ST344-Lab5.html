Background Understanding

explain R0

The basic reproduction number \(R0\) is the expected number of secondary cases generated by a single case in a population where all individuals are susceptible to the disease. This a simplification in the real-world, with infectious diseases including COVID–19 individuals in the population either already have or develop immunity for the disease over time. Effective reproduction number, denoted by \(R\), accounts for this and is the expected number of secondary cases generated by a single case in a population through which the disease is spreading. (Note: The simplest model is discussed further later in this piece.)

The salient point about \(R\) under the simplest model is this: if \(R>1\) then exponential growth in the number of cases will occur; if \(R<1\) then exponential decrease the number of cases will occur. Therefore, how a country deals with a disease in its “first stage” could be the reason for controlling a disease or the reason for succumbing to it.

Producing Plots and Formal Modelling

Firstly, let us plot confirmed cases against time in the UK for the period of 24th February 2020 – 23rd March 2020.

The red regression line in the plot above clearly depicts the increasing trend of COVID–19 cases over the 1 month period but it also depicts the increasing rate of increase of the cases that is, the rate of change of the gradient seems to be increasing. This seems to be exponential so let us explore this further.

Now, to keep it simple we shall examine the simplest model possible, where an individual in the population infects \(R\) individuals at a time \(T\)c after they got infected themselves (the “generation interval”). For COVID-19 we take \(T\)c\(=4\), which a mean quoted by several studies of COVID-19(Note: The distribution of \(R\) in practice is not a point mass citation (Nishiura, Linton, and Akhmetzhanov 2020).)

The distribution for our simplest case is a Dirac delta distribution (a point mass) at \(T\)c. To ensures that the generation interval is not random in this study we give it the the value of \(T\)c state above.

In this study I will take the approach that we model the number I(t) of infected individuals at time t using the equation:

\(I(t) = I(0)*R^{t/}\)\(T\)c

This model for the growth in infections gives a linear model for the log of infections : \(log[I(t)] = log[I(0)] + rt\)

The data are counts which points to the fact that a Poisson distribution with mean rate I(t) would be apt. Since we are using a linear model for the log of this Poisson rate, a Poisson generalized linear model (GLM) is appropriate for the data. Hence, we have a log link function, with the log expectation \(log(𝔼[I∣t])\) of the Poisson being given by \(a+rt\), with parameters \(a\) and \(r\).

To confirm this linear trend we shall plot the log of confirmed cases against time for the period 24th February 2020 – 23rd March 2020.

The data points a clearly noisy but the linear red line suggests that this model is very reasonable and that the rise in cases do indeed follow an exponential growth for this period.
